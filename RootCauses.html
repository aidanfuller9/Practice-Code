Induced Demand

Cause

The term ‘Induced Demand’ regards the idea that there is a constant circle of car dependency, in which the value of different transportation models varies based on the infrastructure of the city. In cities designed around cars, other modes will be less convenient, and vice versa.

Evidence

Induced demand furthers car dependency because in most developed countries, especially in the United States, cities have been constructed to fit cars. This leaves them spread out and most people have no option but to drive places, any other mode would be too time consuming. If cities were more condensed and had better support for walking, cycling, public transportation, etc., people would have more of a choice and cars would not be a requirement for those who cannot or do not want to drive.

Cause

Induced demand is furthered by the ‘Cycle of Automobile Dependency.’ A diagram showing this cycle is provided. It doesn’t have a specific starting point, but rather a complete circle that has spiraled out of control. Increased vehicle ownership leads to specific planning and less variety in options as other modes receive less funding. This leads to large sprawl through the space required for driving and parking, and the cycle repeats.

Evidence

This cycle essentially forces most people to drive places, because walking or biking is too time consuming and public transportation does not have enough routes. Since most towns are constructed to fit this model, most people have no choice but to drive. This is great for some people, but in urban cities there should be more options for commuting.

Car Dependency

Cause

Professor Danial Monsefi Parpari compares the evolution of various worldwide city models, their origins, how each of them contributes to different forms of induced demand. He first comments on a ‘Traditional Walking City,’ a model “restricted to a walkable distance during the age of human…transportation until…the 19th century.” Next, he discusses the more recent layout of cities called the ‘Automobile Dependent City.’ This model really gained influence during the industrial revolution, in which immigrants from all over the world traveled to the United States to escape crowded towns and pursue a more spacious, individual life.

Evidence

While a suburban area is often more peaceful and gives people more space for their families, it commonly causes problems for many who commute daily into more populous cities for work. It is often too time consuming to walk, and public transportation does not have enough specific routes. This forces people to drive instead of any other mode. Some people benefit from vehicles for their jobs, but the majority of people just commute to an office, and they don’t need to carry anything.

Cause

Peter Newman compares four different cities and how they compare in terms of transportation methods. In two ‘Auto Cities,’ Boston and Melbourne, the car dominates the mode of transportation, with very little alternative methods. Over the years, car use continued to rise, while other modes were reduced because of low funding and use. In a ‘Transit City,’ Munich, and a ‘Walking City,’ Singapore, transportation is divided much more equally and provides residents with much more variety in their commute.

Evidence

Cities planned with diversity are much more considerate to all methods of transportation. In car dependent cities, cars dominate transportation while other methods suffer, which forces people to drive. More diverse cities, however, are much more equal, so people have the freedom to decide which one to use.

Probable Solutions

The best solution to enact is better transportation alternatives, such as bus routes and safer pedestrian and bicycle paths. It would be impossible to reduce the size of towns and cities, so the best solution for urban environments is to provide those who need simple transportation to and from work, school, etc. with the means to do so. It is extremely difficult, but somehow the cycle of induced demand will have to be altered to make it less severe. Multiple problems need to be tackled at once, because otherwise it wont have a proper and long lasting impact.
